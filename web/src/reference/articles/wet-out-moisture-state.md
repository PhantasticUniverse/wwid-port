# Wet-Out And Moisture State

## Scope

This page covers condensation, wet-out onset, flue blockage, material/surface
tradeoffs, warming, recovery, cleaning boundaries, early-warning labels, and
wet-state release decisions.

## Agent Summary

Wet-out is not just poor playing technique. It is a coupled result of breath
humidity, instrument temperature, material thermal mass, surface energy,
absorbency, finish, flue geometry, SAC behavior, playing duration, and clearing
method. A material or finish that delays droplets may introduce swelling,
microbial, odor, taste, residue, or cleaning risks. No current local evidence
supports a universal "best wet-out material" claim.

## Maker Workflow

1. Record dry, warming, playing, wet, cleared, and recovered states separately.
2. Measure time-to-onset under declared player/artificial-mouth and environment
   conditions.
3. Capture acoustic early-warning features before gross failure.
4. Record material, finish, flue surface, SAC surface, and cleaning method.
5. Separate condensation control from sanitation and material-safety claims.
6. Use returns to classify wet-out, block drift, leakage, finish swelling, and
   player/environment causes without blame.
7. Treat disassembly, drying, no-heated-drying, refit, and leak-check advice as
   care-state actions that still need material and hygiene compatibility review.

## Data Fields

Useful fields include material, finish, flue geometry, SAC geometry, surface
state, temperature, humidity, instrument temperature, play duration, pressure,
wetness label, onset time, acoustic warning features, failure mode, clearing
method, recovery time, odor/taste observation, cleaning exposure, and release
decision.

## Do Not Overclaim

Do not say absorbent surfaces are simply better. They may delay droplets while
increasing swelling, cracking, microbial retention, cleaning difficulty, or
acoustic drift.

Do not use cleaning or sanitation language without validation for the actual
material system and use context.

Do not fold wet-state performance into dry-state tuning claims. They are
different release states.

## Open Gaps

Needed work includes wet-out response-surface datasets, acoustic early-warning
fixtures, material/finish cleaning compatibility tests, case microclimate
records, and a returned-unit diagnostic ledger that separates block drift,
leakage, flue wetness, finish change, and player/environment causes.
