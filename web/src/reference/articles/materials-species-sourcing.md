# Materials, Species, And Sourcing

## Scope

This page covers wood species, cane, bamboo, elder, sumac, cedar, hardwood,
PVC, ABS, resin, printed bodies, animal-derived materials, metal hardware,
pigments, ethical sourcing, sustainability, reclaimed stock, and source records.

## Agent Summary

The material question is broader than "what sounds good." Species and material
choice can affect moisture movement, cracking, machining, weight, finish
compatibility, scent, allergy/sensory response, wet-out, pitch drift, ethical
sourcing, legal restrictions, and cultural representation. Flutopedia and the
new book sources help identify material traditions and maker vocabulary; the
research reports define the safety and sourcing evidence still needed.

Treat material examples as examples, not approvals. For safety, regulatory,
environmental, or product claims, verify current official and product-specific
evidence before converting a source lead into a requirement.

## Maker Workflow

1. Record material identity before use: species/product, supplier, origin,
   reclaimed status, finish/adhesive compatibility, and contact path.
2. Separate acoustic preference, machining behavior, moisture behavior,
   safety, sourcing, and cultural/source claims.
3. Screen reclaimed, painted, treated, fragrant, unknown, animal-derived,
   metal, pigment, and case materials before they enter breath or lip paths.
4. Capture SDS/TDS, supplier claims, certification, harvest/source evidence,
   and re-review triggers where relevant.
5. Keep exact public claims conservative unless product-specific evidence,
   legal review, and source-governance review support stronger wording.
6. Preserve material substitutions as design changes, not clerical updates.

## Data Fields

Useful fields include material id, common name, scientific name when known,
product name, supplier, lot, origin, harvest/source claim, certification,
reclaimed status, contaminant screen, contact path, density/moisture property
lead, crack-risk lead, machining behavior, dust/fume hazard lead, finish,
adhesive, cure state, animal-derived flag, metal/pigment flag, scent/fragrance
flag, case exposure, rights/CARE status, and public claim tier.

## Do Not Overclaim

Do not rank woods or body materials as universally best for NAFs.

Do not infer safety, sustainability, legality, or ethical sourcing from a
material's natural origin or historical use.

Do not use collection catalog material names as proof of current source,
species, tribe, maker, or safety status.

Do not approve reclaimed stock, animal material, metal hardware, pigments,
fragrant oils, or plastics for breath/lip contact without product-specific
review.
