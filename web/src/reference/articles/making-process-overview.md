# Making Process Overview

## Scope

This page gives agents a manufacturing-process map for NAFs: stock intake,
body/bore method, split-body glue-up, airtightness, block/nest, sound
mechanism, hole layout, finish, voicing/tuning, care release, and shop-safety
handoffs.

## Agent Summary

The Nakai reference book adds practical maker context, including varied wood
choices, two-half construction, glue-up/alignment, airtightness, maker
variation, sound-apparatus vocabulary, condensation care, and trial-and-error
workflow. Use this as descriptive practitioner context, not as a modern safety,
materials, or tuning standard.

The encyclopedia should treat making as a traceable process. A final tuned note
does not erase stock choice, glue-line, bore, block, hole, finish, wet-state,
or inspection history.

## Maker Workflow

1. Record stock identity, source, moisture state, defects, and contact-path
   review before cutting.
2. Record body method: split tube, drilled/bored tube, routed halves, printed
   body, or other construction.
3. Capture bore, wall, glue-line, alignment, and airtightness checks before
   sound-mechanism work hides upstream variation.
4. Record block/nest/sound-mechanism geometry and vocabulary used by the maker.
5. Record hole layout method and whether it is body-derived, calculator-derived,
   measured-copy, WIDesigner-derived, or empirical.
6. Preserve as-designed, as-built, as-voiced, as-finished, and as-released
   states.
7. Hand off oil, finish, cleaning, drying, case, and shop exposure claims to
   the materials and care pages.

## Data Fields

Useful fields include stock species/product, stock source, construction method,
body halves, bore method, glue, adhesive cure, alignment, airtightness, bore
diameter, wall thickness, block/nest terminology, spacer/edge material, flue
geometry, TSH geometry, hole layout source, hole stations, hole diameters,
finish, care action, voicing changes, measured pitch, pressure, environment,
inspection state, and release decision.

## Do Not Overclaim

Do not use the book to rank woods, approve finishes, approve oils, approve
PVC/case materials, or validate body-measure hole spacing.

Do not turn descriptive historical or practitioner methods into equal-tempered,
calculator-backed, or production-release rules.

Do not omit shop-safety, SDS/TDS, dust, ventilation, fire, adhesive, solvent,
and PPE review because a source describes handcraft practice.
